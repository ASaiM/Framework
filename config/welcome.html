ASaiM (Auvergne Sequence analysis of intestinal Microbiota) is an environment to analyze microbiota, with a focus on intestinal microbiota. Read more about ASaiM context

This environment is composed of an open-source opiniated framework, based on a custom Galaxy instance. This framework integrates numerous tools for data pretreatments, taxonomic analyses and functional analyses, and chosen databases dedicated to process microbiota data. Several standard but customizable workflows are also proposed to help the analysis of microbial data.

For more information about the tools, databases, workflows and ASaiM framework in general, please read the ASaiM documentation. You can also follow our Tutorial to learn how to use the ASaiM framework to analyze microbiota data.

Galaxy is an open, web-based platform for data intensive biomedical research. The Galaxy team is a part of BX at Penn State, and the Biology department at Johns Hopkins University. The Galaxy Project is supported in part by NHGRI, NSF, The Huck Institutes of the Life Sciences, The Institute for CyberScience at Penn State, and Johns Hopkins.
